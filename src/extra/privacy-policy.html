PRIVACY POLICY

Last updated: 4-August-2023

This Privacy Policy includes important information about your personal data and we encourage you to read it carefully along with our Sensitive Information Policy.

Welcome

This document sets out the Privacy Policy (Policy) of Smile Connect Pty Ltd (ACN 669 116 081) and its authorised or licensed entities and health services providers which make reference or link to this Policy, as well as of Life Smiles®, Sleep Dentistry Group®, Dental Implants Masters, All On 4 Clinic, All On 4 Plus Pty Ltd (ACN 164844428) and its network of clinics and licensees trading as All-On-4 Plus® Premium Provider and All-On-4 Plus® Affiliates, each individually an “Organisation” (as relevant and subject to appropriate context) and collectively a “Network”.

When we talk about “we,” “our,” or “us” in this Policy, we are referring to the specific Organisation which is relevant to you, or with which you engaged or shared your Personal Data, and/or the specific Organisation which provided a platform to enable you to share your Personal Data.

This Policy describes the “Personal Data” that we collect about you, how we use it, how we share it, your rights and choices, and how you can contact us about our privacy practices.

“Personal Data” means any information that we collect about you or which relates to you including information about how you engage with our website, platform or services.

  1. Extent of Personal Date Collection

    USING OUR ONLINE SERVICES

    When you use our websites, portals, apps or any digital platform (Online Services) we may collect personal information about you (such as name, email address, phone number) as well as information on how you engage with our website, platform or services, for example device information, time of use, IP address, etc.

    We use Cookies (small text files that are stored in a computer’s browser directory) and other similar technologies to:

    • Ensure that our Online Services function properly;
    • Detect and prevent fraud
    • Help us understand how you use and engage with our Online Services
    • Analyse and improve our Online Services and our other services which we may offer you from time to time; and
    • Help you access our Online Services such as remembering your login details and storing your preferences.

    Your web browser may allow you to manage your cookie preferences, including to delete and disable cookies. You can take a look at the help section of your web browser to understand your options. If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our Site or Services may not operate as intended.

    CONTACTING US

    When making appointment or visiting one of our Organisations we will collect and store the following information about you:

    1. Your Name
    2. Date Of Birth
    3. Dental Insurance Fund
    4. Contact Details
    5. Address Details
    6. The nature of the problem you are seeking treatment for
    7. Investigations and advice you have been given during your treatment
    8. The clinic you are being treated at
    9. Details of your enquiry
    10. Treatments and procedure being performed, clinician/s name/s, and treatment dates
    11. Other information described in our Sensitive Information Policy

    SEEKING TREATMENT

    When considering or opting for treatment at one of our Organisations we will collect and store the following information about you:

    1. Employer name and contact details
    2. Drivers Licence
    3. Next of Kin (Emergency Contact) name and contact number
    4. Medical practitioner details name and contact details
    5. Your medical history including allergies, conditions, recent health, medications, artificial body parts, social factors, dental history, health complaints, fitness level,
    6. Details of the person picking you up after surgery

    APPLYING FOR FINANCE

    When considering or applying for finance at one of our Organisations we will collect and store the following information about you:

    1. Rates Notice showing the value of property
    2. The current home loan balance
    3. name and address of all owners
    4. loan statement
    5. Drivers license/passport information

  2. Why is information collected?

    We collect personal and health information to enable us to assess your individual circumstances and to provide you with appropriate treatment, goods and services and best possible care.

    We may also use your information for clinical, scientific or health research and analysis, including for publication purposes, as well as for market research and analysis.

  3. How do we use the information about you?

    1. Your health information will be treated with the utmost confidentiality. With the exception of disclosure to persons and organisations under this Policy and our Sensitive Information Policy, disclosure will not otherwise be made to any other person not involved in either your treatment or the administration of the Organisation; or any person who is NOT a participant of a training and education program and/or involved in a clinical study or research project without the Patient’s prior consent.

    2. We use your information to contact you to:

      • Discuss details about your treatment, condition or any other matters we deem relevant to your care
      • Check pre-operative information
      • To inform you of about expenses
      • Follow up on your recovery, journey or experience
    3. Personal information such as your name, address and health insurance details will be sent to your health fund and used for the purpose of addressing accounts to you, as well as processing payments and writing to you about our services and any issues affecting you or your treatment.
    4. General information about your condition may be provided to your next of kin or a near relative unless you request otherwise.
    5. We may share your Personal Data and your health information with collaborators within the Network, as well as third party collaborators outside the Network, who are relevant to your care or involved in your assessment, administration or care (directly or indirectly).
    6. We may share with your General Practitioner (GP) and/or other healthcare practitioners health information about your condition and/or treatment and may include information such as your medication and any special instructions. We may also require health information from your GP and/or other healthcare practitioners if, in our judgement, that is necessary in the context of your treatment. If you do not wish any of this information to be shared please inform us by writing to the Privacy Officer below as soon as possible.
    7. A hospital, community health provider, or doctor may contact us to obtain information about you so that they can continue your ongoing care. Please inform us as soon as possible (in writing) if you would like to request that information not be provided to a particular person or organisation.
    8. we may also use parts of your health information and records for research purposes, in study groups or at seminars as this may provide benefit to other patients or professional colleagues. Should that happen, your personal identity will not be disclosed without consent to do so.
    9. The legislation requires that certain information about patients who have specific conditions and treatments must be reported to databases or registers maintained by the Department of Health or other health organisations. Patient information recorded on the databases and registers is kept strictly confidential.
    10. There may be occasions where we are obligated by or authorised under, law to disclose patient information, such as the presentation of your medical record to a court when subpoenaed in the case of legal action.

  4. Destruction of information about you

    The information we collect about you can only be destroyed after a specified time period. All patient records are kept for a minimum of seven years as per legislation.

  5. Accessing and correcting information about you

    1. If you are seeking access to records regarding your attendances at you dentist/doctor’s suite, please contact the dentist/doctor‘s rooms directly.

    2. Your medical history, treatment records, x-rays and any other material relevant to the your treatment will be kept by us onsite for a period of four (4) years, and will be archived off site for a further period as required by law. You may inspect or request copies of the records of treatment at any time, or seek an explanation from us. Statutory fees will apply in relation to the types of access you seek. If you request an explanation of our records or a written summary, the following fees will apply:

      1. Admin fee $100 plus $150 per hour per person (reception and general staff); plus
      2. $450 per hour per clinician, if required (dentist and AHPRA-registered health care providers)
    3. If you want more information about your care and treatment you must speak to your doctor or appropriate healthcare providers.
    4. If you would like a copy of your health information, or to correct or update your information (including health information) you will need to complete and send to us the Request for Access to Information form along with a copy of your photo identification. There is a charge for this service typically between $100 and $350.
    5. If you believe that any of the information that we hold about you is incorrect, inaccurate, or misleading, please contact the Privacy Officer for further details regarding how to amend or correct your health information.
